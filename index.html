I am a research associate in climate risk analytics associated with the UK Centre for Greening Finance and Investment. My research focuses on compound wind and flood risks, historically and in the future.

I have now been at the University of Bristol for one year! Before this I spent eight years working at the University of Reading studying the impacts of climate variability and climate change on national-level power systems including Europe, Mexico, and multiple regions of Africa. A key outcome of my work has been to improve the accessibility of large meteorological datasets to non-specialists within the energy, insurance, and financial sectors. Hannah is also a member of the 'next generation challenges in energy and climate modelling' group, which organises events to promote collaboration in the energy-meteorology communities. I am passionate about science communication and have been involved in multiple international events to engage schools and the general public in climate science. .
